📊 Business Insights
1.Customer deposits are concentrated among a relatively small segment of customers, indicating opportunities for targeted relationship management.

2.Savings and checking accounts account for the majority of customer accounts, making them the bank's primary products.

3.Customers with higher credit scores generally qualify for larger loan amounts, reflecting standard lending practices.

4.Certain cities contribute a larger share of total deposits and loans, making them strategically important markets.

5.Loan demand varies across customer segments, suggesting opportunities for tailored lending products.

6.A subset of customers owns multiple banking products (accounts, cards, and loans), demonstrating stronger customer engagement.

7.Credit score segmentation helps identify customers with higher lending potential as well as those requiring closer risk assessment.

8.Deposit distribution is uneven, with high-value customers contributing significantly to the bank's total deposits.

9.Average interest rates remain relatively consistent across the loan portfolio, supporting stable lending policies.

10.Integrating customer, account, loan, and card data provides a comprehensive view of customer relationships and product utilization.
